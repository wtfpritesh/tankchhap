Our Story · Since 1949

A legacy built since 1949

टैंक छाप — तीन पीढ़ियों की पहचान

In the story of Indian rainwear, few names carry the weight of trust, resilience, and generational legacy quite like Tank Chhap — a heritage umbrella and raincoat cloth brand built across three generations of the Lodha family in Ratlam, Indore, and Mumbai.

Generation One · 1949

Founded in Ratlam by Shri Mangilal Ji Kesrimal Ji Lodha

The promise that started everything

Tank Chhap was founded in Ratlam in 1949 by Shri Mangilal Ji Kesrimal Ji Lodha, not merely as a business, but as a commitment — a promise that dependable protection from rain and weather should never be compromised. At a time when quality, trust, and craftsmanship were the true currency of reputation, the foundation was laid for what would become one of India's most recognised names in umbrellas, raincoat cloth, and rainwear.

From its earliest days, Tank Chhap stood for durability, reliability, and consistency. It was built on a simple but powerful principle: a product should earn trust not through words, but through years of performance. That philosophy transformed Tank Chhap from a regional enterprise in Ratlam into a respected and growing force across central India.

Build quality products, earn trust, and grow through consistency — the working philosophy laid down in 1949 that still defines every Tank Chhap umbrella and every metre of raincoat cloth that leaves the workshop today.
Generation Two · Expansion

Carried forward by Shri Prakash Ji Lodha

Son of the founder · Architect of expansion

As the legacy strengthened, Shri Prakash Ji Lodha, son of Shri Mangilal Ji Kesrimal Ji Lodha, carried his father's vision forward with extraordinary dedication. Following firmly in his father's footsteps while bringing his own strategic foresight, he expanded Tank Chhap exponentially — transforming it from a trusted foundation into a far-reaching enterprise.

Through relentless commitment, deep market understanding, and product expertise built lot after lot, Shri Prakash Ji Lodha broadened Tank Chhap's reach, strengthened its presence, and helped establish it as a powerful name across wider markets. Under his stewardship, Tank Chhap evolved beyond a product. It became a household identity — associated with dependable rain protection, quality craftsmanship, and generational trust.

Generation Three · National Scale

Led today by Pinkesh Lodha

Grandson of the founder · Owner, Tank Chhap

Today, Pinkesh Lodha — son of Shri Prakash Ji Lodha and grandson of Shri Mangilal Ji Kesrimal Ji Lodha — stands at the helm of Tank Chhap, leading the brand into its boldest era yet. Representing the next chapter of this historic journey, Pinkesh Lodha has taken Tank Chhap beyond legacy and into national prominence.

Through vision, scale, and modern expansion, he has grown the business across the entire country — transforming Tank Chhap into a true house-name brand recognised by customers, retailers, and rainwear buyers across India. Under his leadership, Tank Chhap continues to evolve while preserving the principles that built it.

From Ratlam to India-wide recognition, Tank Chhap today stands as a symbol of how family legacy, when guided with vision, can become national identity.

More than a brand — a house name

For decades, Tank Chhap has not simply sold umbrellas and raincoat cloth. It has built familiarity across generations of Indian buyers. For many, Tank Chhap is remembered as:

  • A trusted umbrella & raincoat cloth manufacturer
  • A reliable rainwear name across India
  • A family legacy spanning three generations
  • A symbol of lasting quality and craftsmanship
  • A dependable choice across every monsoon
  • A heritage brand from the BSC Umbrellas house

That is what separates Tank Chhap from ordinary market presence. It is not just a product line — it is a brand shaped by over seven decades of trust.

Tank Chhap today

From its founding in Ratlam in 1949 to its national growth today, Tank Chhap remains rooted in the values that first defined it: build quality products, earn trust, and grow through consistency.

With a foundation laid by Shri Mangilal Ji Kesrimal Ji Lodha, expanded by Shri Prakash Ji Lodha, and nationally scaled by Pinkesh Lodha, Tank Chhap continues to strengthen its place as one of India's enduring rainwear legacies.
